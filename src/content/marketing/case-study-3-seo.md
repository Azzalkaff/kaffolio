## Problem Framing
A B2B SaaS company had a beautiful Next.js application but absolutely zero organic visibility on Google. They relied entirely on expensive paid ads (SEM) to acquire users. As their ad budget bloated, their Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) became unsustainable. They needed a long-term, compounding organic acquisition channel.

## Objective & KPI
- **Primary KPI:** Grow organic search traffic from 0 to 30,000 monthly visitors within 12 months.
- **Secondary KPI:** Generate high-intent leads without ad spend.
- **Goal:** Build an automated programmatic SEO architecture combined with high-quality editorial content.

## Strategy
We engineered a **hybrid SEO engine**:
- **Technical Foundation:** Fixing Next.js rendering bottlenecks that were preventing Googlebot from indexing dynamic pages.
- **Programmatic Pages:** Creating scalable template pages for "Alternative to X" and "X vs Y" comparison keywords.
- **Editorial Content:** Publishing deep-dive technical tutorials for developers (the core audience).

## Execution
1. **Technical SEO Audit:** Optimized Core Web Vitals, generated dynamic XML sitemaps using Next.js App Router, and fixed hydration errors.
2. **Keyword Clustering:** Identified low-competition, high-intent BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) keywords related to their SaaS niche using Ahrefs.
3. **Content Engine:** Hired 3 freelance technical writers to produce 8 high-quality, long-form blog posts per month that solved specific developer problems.
4. **Internal Linking:** Built a robust programmatic internal linking structure using semantic silos.

## Learnings
The most impactful decision was targeting BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) comparison keywords first (e.g., "Best API gateway for Node.js") rather than broad TOFU (Top of Funnel) terms. Even though search volumes were smaller, the conversion rate to leads was 10x higher. Quality traffic always trumps vanity traffic in B2B SaaS.
